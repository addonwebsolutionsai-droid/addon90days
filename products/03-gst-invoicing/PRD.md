## 1. Problem

India has 14 million GST-registered businesses. Every single one of them faces the same compliance burden, every single month, without exception.

**The monthly GST compliance cycle (what every business goes through):**

1. Collect all sales invoices for the month.
2. Collect all purchase invoices (from suppliers).
3. Reconcile sales against GSTR-2B (auto-generated by the portal from supplier filings).
4. Identify mismatches (supplier filed differently than what you received — affects Input Tax Credit).
5. Fill GSTR-1 (outward supplies — all sales invoices with customer GSTIN, invoice number, HSN codes, amounts, tax breakdown).
6. File GSTR-3B (summary return — total output tax, total ITC claimed, tax payable, payment).
7. Repeat next month. Plus GSTR-9 annual return at year-end.

**What actually happens in a typical SMB:**

- **Using a CA:** 80%+ of small businesses outsource this to a Chartered Accountant. Cost: ₹1,500–₹5,000/month just for filing. The CA asks the business owner to "WhatsApp me all your invoices" every month. Owner scrambles to collect paper invoices from drawers, photographs them, sends 40 photos in a WhatsApp chat. CA manually enters data into Tally or ClearTax portal. Takes 3–4 days. Meanwhile ITC is being claimed incorrectly because the CA doesn't have time to reconcile each invoice against GSTR-2B. Penalty notices arrive 6–18 months later.
- **Using Tally:** Complex, desktop-only, ₹18,000/year, requires a Tally-trained operator. Data entry is still manual. Generating GSTR-1 from Tally requires additional steps and the GST module. Not accessible to a freelancer or small trader who works on a phone.
- **Using ClearTax / Zoho Books:** Better than Tally for filing, but: not AI-native, invoice OCR is basic (doesn't understand messy handwritten or printed Indian invoices), no proactive mismatch alerts, no notice-response drafting, expensive for small businesses (₹4,000–₹12,000/year just for GST filing on ClearTax).
- **Manual data entry into the GST portal:** Extremely tedious. Session timeouts, CAPTCHA, slow portal. Many small traders just give up and hire someone to do it on the day before the deadline — creating last-minute panic every month.

**What the problem actually costs:**
- CA fees: ₹2,000–₹5,000/month = ₹24,000–₹60,000/year.
- Late filing penalty: ₹50–₹200 per day of delay on GSTR-3B (₹1,500–₹3,000 minimum for a few days late).
- Interest on unpaid tax: 18% per annum.
- ITC loss from unreconciled mismatches: often ₹10,000–₹50,000/year for small businesses.
- Penalty notices from GST department for discrepancies: ₹10,000–₹1,00,000+.

**The buyer profile:**

- **Freelancers and consultants:** Graphic designers, IT consultants, marketing consultants. GST-registered because their turnover exceeds ₹20L threshold. Filing 10–50 invoices/month. Currently using a CA because they don't understand GST. Will happily pay ₹499–₹999/mo if the tool is simple enough that they don't need the CA.
- **Small traders and retailers:** Shopkeepers, distributors, small manufacturers. 50–500 invoices/month. Larger compliance burden. Currently the biggest source of business for small CAs. Will pay ₹1,499–₹2,999/mo if reconciliation is automated.
- **E-commerce sellers:** Amazon/Flipkart/Meesho sellers. Get GST invoices from platform plus they issue invoices. Compliance is complex due to B2C vs. B2B mix. Currently either CA or struggling alone.
- **CAs and Tax Professionals:** Don't just sell to end businesses — sell to CAs who manage 20–100 client accounts. TaxPilot becomes their professional tool. One CA sign-up = 20–100 end business accounts.

## 2. Solution

TaxPilot is an AI-first GST compliance and invoicing platform. The core thesis: everything that can be automated (data extraction, HSN code lookup, GSTR population, mismatch detection) should be automated using AI, leaving the business owner or their CA to review and approve — not to manually enter data.

### Core product modules

**Invoice Generation (GST-compliant)**
- Create a GST invoice in under 60 seconds: select customer (from contacts), add line items (item name → auto-suggest HSN/SAC code from a 5,000-item HSN database → auto-fill GST rate), system calculates CGST/SGST/IGST based on customer's state.
- Invoice auto-numbering (configurable format: INV-2024-001, or FY-based INV-24-25-001).
- Templates: 5 professional invoice templates (minimal, classic, branded). Upload company logo.
- PDF generation: download as PDF, share via WhatsApp, email via Resend.
- Recurring invoices: set up a subscription customer → invoice auto-generated on configured date every month → sent automatically.
- Credit notes and debit notes.
- Proforma invoices (not tax invoices — for quotations).
- Invoice in multiple languages: English, Hindi, Gujarati, Tamil, Telugu (customer name and address rendered in regional script).

**Expense & Purchase Invoice Upload**
- Upload method 1 — Photo (Claude Vision): Business owner photographs a supplier invoice (thermal print, laser print, handwritten). Claude extracts: supplier name, GSTIN, invoice number, date, line items with quantities and rates, HSN codes, GST amounts (CGST/SGST/IGST). User reviews extracted data on a side-by-side view (photo on left, extracted fields on right), corrects if needed, saves.
- Upload method 2 — WhatsApp bot: "Send me your invoice" → user forwards the invoice image to TaxPilot's WhatsApp number → auto-processed via same Claude Vision flow → confirmation message sent back: "Invoice from [Supplier] for ₹[amount] captured. Review at app.taxpilot.in."
- Upload method 3 — Email: Forward invoice emails (PDF attachments) to a dedicated inbox (e.g., expenses@[company].taxpilot.in) → PDF extracted → Claude processes.
- Upload method 4 — Bulk CSV import (for businesses migrating from Tally/Zoho).
- Expense categorization: each invoice auto-categorized (office supplies, travel, professional services, raw materials) using Claude. User can override category.

**GSTR-1 Auto-Population**
- All sales invoices for the month → automatically organized into GSTR-1 format: B2B invoices (with customer GSTIN), B2C large (>₹2.5L), B2C small, exports, nil-rated, exempt.
- HSN summary section auto-generated from invoice line items.
- Table 11 (tax liability): auto-calculated from all invoices.
- Preview the complete GSTR-1 before filing — line-by-line review.
- One-click filing to GST portal via NIC API (official GST government API — sandbox available for testing, production requires GSTIN-level authentication via DSC or EVC/OTP).
- Filing status tracking: pending → filed → acknowledgment received (ARN).
- Amendment handling: if an invoice was filed incorrectly, create amendment in GSTR-1A.

**GSTR-3B Preview and Mismatch Alerts**
- System auto-calculates GSTR-3B values from: output tax (from sales invoices) + ITC (from purchase invoices) + adjustments.
- GSTR-2B reconciliation: download GSTR-2B from portal (via API or manual JSON upload) → compare each supplier invoice in our system against GSTR-2B → highlight mismatches.
- Mismatch categories: "Supplier filed but you didn't enter" (missed ITC claim), "You entered but supplier didn't file" (ITC at risk, may need to reverse), "Amount mismatch" (supplier filed different amount).
- Actionable alerts: "You have ₹12,450 in unclaimed ITC. Match these 3 invoices." — click-through to the specific invoices.
- ITC calculation with Section 17(5) blocked credit detection: certain purchases (cars, food, entertainment) are blocked for ITC — system auto-flags and excludes.
- Tax payable calculation: output tax − eligible ITC = cash payment required.
- Payment challan generation: pre-filled PMT-06 values for payment on GST portal.

**One-Click GST Filing**
- Authentication: GSTIN + OTP (EVC) for small taxpayers. DSC (Digital Signature Certificate) for large taxpayers (DSC file upload supported).
- Filing sequence: file GSTR-1 first (by 11th of month) → then GSTR-3B (by 20th).
- Filing reminders: push notification 5 days before deadline, 2 days before, day of.
- Filing history: archived returns with ARN numbers, date filed, tax paid.
- For CA mode: file on behalf of client (CA's login credentials used, client GSTIN selected from managed list).

**AI-Drafted GST Notice Replies**
- User uploads a GST notice (PDF or photograph) → Claude extracts: notice type, demand amount, assessment period, issues raised, response deadline.
- Claude drafts a professional reply: formal tone, addresses each issue, references relevant GST provisions (Input-Output mismatch explanations, ITC reversal justification, etc.).
- User reviews draft, edits, downloads as PDF. Files directly or gives to CA.
- Notice tracker: all notices with status (received, replied, pending, resolved, appealed).
- This feature alone is worth the subscription to most small business owners — a CA charges ₹5,000–₹20,000 for a single notice reply.

**HSN/SAC Code Lookup AI**
- Business owner types what they sell: "I sell handmade leather wallets" → Claude suggests the correct HSN code (4205 90 99) and applicable GST rate (12%), with confidence score and explanation.
- Bulk HSN suggestion: import product list (CSV) → Claude processes each item → returns suggested HSN + rate.
- HSN code database: complete 8-digit HSN master maintained and updated (GST Council rate changes are reflected within 24 hours of notification).

**E-Invoice (IRN generation)**
- Mandatory for businesses with turnover >₹5 crore (from October 2022) and gradually being extended.
- TaxPilot generates e-invoice JSON per IRP (Invoice Registration Portal) schema.
- Submits to IRP via API → receives IRN (Invoice Reference Number) + QR code.
- IRN QR code embedded in the invoice PDF automatically.
- Bulk IRN generation for multiple invoices.

**CA Collaboration Mode**
- Business owner invites their CA to their TaxPilot workspace.
- CA gets a separate login with access to: all invoices, GSTR drafts, notice inbox.
- CA can file on behalf of the client directly from TaxPilot.
- CA can add notes/comments on specific invoices.
- Role: CA has full read + file access but cannot delete invoices.

**CA Professional Dashboard (multi-client)**
- CA signs up on TaxPilot and manages all client accounts from one dashboard.
- Client list with compliance status: GSTR-1 filed / not filed, GSTR-3B due date countdown, ITC reconciliation status.
- Bulk operations: download all clients' GSTR-2B reconciliation reports in one click.
- Client monthly summary report: auto-generated PDF with all GST activity, sent to client automatically.
- Pricing: CA pays ₹499/mo per client slot (or bundle pricing) → sells the service to client at whatever rate they choose. This is how we capture the CA channel.

### What TaxPilot is NOT (v1 scope)
- Not a full accounting system (no balance sheet, no P&L, no bank reconciliation — these require a dedicated accounting tool like Zoho Books or Tally).
- Not a payroll system (TDS/EPF/ESI filing is out of scope).
- Not a direct tax (income tax) filing tool — focused on GST only.
- Not a legal/CA replacement — always recommend users consult a CA for complex situations. We are a tool, not a professional service.

## 7. Risks & mitigations

**Risk 1: NIC GST API is unreliable and poorly documented**
- The government's GST portal and APIs are notoriously unreliable — downtime spikes during deadline periods (around the 11th and 20th of every month when lakhs of businesses are filing simultaneously).
- **Mitigation:** Build with resilience: all filing operations use BullMQ with retry logic (up to 5 retries, exponential backoff). Show users real-time portal status (we poll the portal's status page). Fallback: always allow JSON export for manual upload to the portal if API filing fails. Never tell a user "filing complete" until we have the ARN — always "filing in progress, will confirm." Dead-letter queue for failed filings with manual resolution workflow.

**Risk 2: Legal risk — advice vs. tool distinction**
- If a user incorrectly claims ITC based on our reconciliation and gets audited, they may blame us.
- **Mitigation:** Clear ToS: TaxPilot is a tool, not a professional service. "Always consult a CA for compliance decisions." Disclaimer on all reports: "This report is generated from data you provided. Verify with your Chartered Accountant before filing." Notice reply tool: "This draft is for guidance only. Have it reviewed by a qualified CA before submitting to the GST department." This is a legal protection, not a cop-out — it's the standard for every tax software globally.

**Risk 3: Claude Vision OCR errors on messy Indian invoices**
- Indian suppliers often use thermal printers with faded ink, handwritten invoices, non-standard formats, and regional language text.
- **Mitigation:** Always show a side-by-side review UI — user must confirm extracted data before it saves. Never auto-save without review. Build a feedback loop: when users correct extracted data, log the correction (without PII). Use corrections to improve prompts. Use Claude Sonnet (not Haiku) for OCR — higher accuracy worth the cost on a per-invoice basis (₹0.02–0.05 per invoice is negligible vs. subscription revenue).

**Risk 4: GST regulations change frequently**
- GST Council meets multiple times per year and changes rates, rules, filing deadlines.
- **Mitigation:** Subscribe to GST Council press releases via RSS. Maintain an internal "GST changes log." When a rate change happens, update HSN rate table within 24 hours. Build the system so GST rates are data (database), not code — changing rates doesn't require a code deployment. CA advisors in our beta network will flag changes proactively.

**Risk 5: ClearTax competitive response**
- ClearTax has massive scale (5 million+ users) and could add AI features.
- **Mitigation:** ClearTax's strength is also their weakness — 5 million users means they can't change UX dramatically. We win on three specific things they can't easily replicate: (1) WhatsApp-native invoice capture (culturally Indian, instant workflow), (2) CA collaboration model that's designed around how Indian CAs actually work (WhatsApp-based client management), (3) Claude-powered notice reply (they have a basic notice tracker, not AI drafting). We don't need to beat them — we need to own a specific niche (small traders, freelancers, and the CA channel) better than they do.

**Risk 6: E-invoice IRP API production access requires GSTIN-level registration**
- The IRP API for e-invoice generation requires the taxpayer (or their authorized representative) to be registered as an ASP (Application Service Provider) or GSP (GST Suvidha Provider).
- **Mitigation:** Apply for GSP registration (process takes 2–4 weeks, cost minimal). In the interim: generate e-invoice JSON in IRP format → user can upload directly to IRP portal. This covers the gap without blocking the launch. E-invoice is a Growth+ feature, not available on Solo tier, so it affects a smaller portion of early users.

**Security specifics (tax data is sensitive):**
- All invoice data encrypted at rest (Supabase AES-256).

- Founder personally reaches out to 50 CAs in Ahmedabad via LinkedIn + personal network. Ahmedabad has a dense CA community (ICAI Ahmedabad chapter has 8,000+ members).
- Pitch: "Save 20 hours/month on GST reconciliation. Manage all your clients from one dashboard. We'll set it up for free for your first 5 clients."
- Offer: First 3 months at ₹199/client (normally ₹499). CA keeps the tool if they bring at least 5 clients.
- Target: 10 CAs signed up in Month 1, each managing 10 clients average = 100 end-business accounts.

*Channel 2: ICAI events and study circles*
- ICAI (Institute of Chartered Accountants of India) Gujarat chapter has monthly study circles and seminars.
- Present TaxPilot at one study circle meeting: "How AI is changing GST compliance practice." Not a sales pitch — a useful presentation on AI in accounting. Product mention at the end.
- Build relationships with CA community leaders.

*Channel 3: CA WhatsApp groups*
- CA WhatsApp groups in Gujarat are extremely active (hundreds of CAs sharing notices, rate change updates, FAQs).
- Join 5–10 groups. Build reputation by sharing useful GST updates first (3–4 weeks). Then share TaxPilot demo video.
- This is the highest-converting channel for CA adoption.

**Month 2 — Direct SMB + digital**

*Channel 4: LinkedIn content (GST tips)*
- Founder posts daily: "GST tip of the day", "How to check if your supplier filed GSTR-1 before you claim ITC", "Notice DRC-01: what it means and how to reply."
- Each post ends with: "We built TaxPilot so small businesses can handle this without a CA. Try free."
- LinkedIn targeting: GST-registered business owners, small business owners in India.

*Channel 5: Google Ads (high intent)*
- Keywords: "gst filing software india", "gst return filing online", "gst software for small business", "gst reconciliation tool", "gst notice reply format".
- These keywords have strong commercial intent — person searching is actively looking to solve a compliance problem.
- Budget: ₹30,000–₹50,000/mo.
- Landing page: per problem type (freelancer filing, small trader filing, CA multi-client).

*Channel 6: YouTube content*
- "How to file GSTR-1 yourself in 15 minutes" — tutorial video using TaxPilot.
- "I got a GST notice — here's what I did" — real story, shows notice reply feature.
- "Stop paying ₹3,000/month to your CA for basic GST filing" — provocative, targets freelancers.

**Month 3 — Referral + expansion**

*Channel 7: Referral program*
- End-user referral: refer a business → both get 1 month free.
- CA referral: CA refers another CA → ₹2,000 credit per new CA who activates 5+ clients.
- The CA referral is particularly powerful: CAs know all other CAs, and they share tools they trust enthusiastically.

*Channel 8: GSTIN verification API (B2B SaaS)*
- Expose a GSTIN verification and invoice data API.
- Target: SaaS companies, e-commerce platforms, ERP vendors who need GSTIN validation.
- Usage-based pricing: ₹0.10 per GSTIN lookup, ₹0.50 per invoice OCR API call.
- This is a small revenue stream but drives brand awareness in the developer community.

**Pricing psychology:**
- ₹499/mo is psychologically "less than one CA visit." That comparison is explicitly made in all marketing.
- Annual billing discount eliminates monthly churn friction for compliance tools (set it up in April, pay for the year, forget about it).
- CA Professional pricing is per client — the variable cost model matches how CAs think (I have 30 clients, so I pay for 30).
