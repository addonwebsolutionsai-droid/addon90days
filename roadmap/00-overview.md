# Roadmap Overview

**The strategic arc of 90 days.** For day-by-day execution, see `daily-runbook.md`.

## The one-line version

From dying custom dev shop → AI-native product company with $25k–$75k MRR and multiple enterprise conversations in 90 days.

## Phase structure

### Phase 1: Foundation (Days 1-30)
**Goal:** Stand up the agent workforce. Ship first revenue products (Claude Toolkit + ChatBase). Rebrand. Land first paying customers.

Key milestones:
- Day 1-7: All 13 agents operational
- Day 15: Claude Toolkit (IoT Skills Pack) public (first revenue)
- Day 16: Indian Business Stack MCP live
- Day 24: AI Company Starter launched
- Day 28: Re-engagement emails to past clients
- Day 30: ChatBase public launch

End-of-phase metric: first paid customers, $500-$2k MRR, 20-50 active pipeline.

### Phase 2: Launch (Days 31-60)
**Goal:** Launch more products. Scale content + outbound. First enterprise IoT pitches. Reach $10k MRR.

Key milestones:
- Week 5: ChatBase paid customers acquiring
- Week 6: Content engine at 30 pieces/week
- Week 6: TaxPilot decision (build or defer)
- Week 7: First enterprise IoT × AI discovery calls
- Week 8: First enterprise proposal in motion

End-of-phase metric: $3k-$10k MRR, 25-75 paying customers, 3+ enterprise calls, $50k-$200k pipeline.

### Phase 3: Scale (Days 61-90)
**Goal:** Kill losers. Double down on winners. Close enterprise. Build systems for Month 4+.

Key milestones:
- Week 9: Brutal triage — reallocate 70% of capacity to top 2 performers
- Week 10: First enterprise contract signed
- Week 11: Documentation sprint for future human hires
- Week 12: Public case study, Month 4-12 plan committed

End-of-phase metric: $25k-$75k MRR (realistic), $150k+ (stretch), 100+ customers, 1-3 enterprise contracts, founder time <90 min/day operational.

## Revenue pillar allocation

Across 90 days, budget your attention roughly:

| Pillar | Share of attention | Why |
|---|---|---|
| 1. SaaS micro-products | 35% | Biggest volume opportunity |
| 2. Claude ecosystem | 20% | Fastest time to revenue |
| 3. IoT × AI enterprise | 30% | Our moat; biggest contracts |
| 4. Productized services | 15% | Fills cash gaps, leverages existing capability |

This isn't fixed — it shifts based on what's working. At Day 60, the mix might be 50% on the two products making money and 50% on everything else.

## Key assumptions

- Founder executes ~90 min/day of operational oversight, plus 4-6 hrs/week of strategic work and sales calls
- Claude API burn caps at $5k/month — system throttles if approaching
- No hiring in 90 days (all work via agents)
- Existing past-client relationships are leveraged (not cold-starting the network)

## Kill-switch days

Three days where we brutally evaluate and kill what's not working:

- **Day 28:** Phase 1 retrospective. Kill any product/channel/agent not showing life.
- **Day 56:** Phase 2 retrospective. Second major pruning.
- **Day 90:** Phase 3 retrospective. Set Year-1 plan.

## The four things that must be true at Day 90

1. **Revenue:** at least $25k MRR across the portfolio (stretch $75k)
2. **Durable winners:** at least 2 products clearly earning their place and growing weekly
3. **Enterprise signal:** at least 1 enterprise contract signed or very-late-stage
4. **Systems:** the 13-agent factory runs with <90 min/day from founder

If all four are true: continue and scale.
If three are true: course-correct, mostly continue.
If two or fewer: hard reset on strategy.

## Beyond Day 90

The Day 90 case study ("How we ran a 13-agent AI company for 90 days") becomes a perennial lead generator. The documented system becomes the template for:
- Hiring first humans (sales closer, ops manager — the two roles agents are weakest at)
- Expanding the portfolio (adding products 8-10 in Months 4-6)
- Licensing or franchising the AI factory template to other founders (Claude Toolkit evolution)

But that's future work. Days 1-90 is about making the factory WORK first.
