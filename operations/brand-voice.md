# Brand Voice — AddonWebSolutions

**The house style. Every piece of content and every piece of customer communication follows this.**

## Our voice in one sentence

We are engineers who ship — direct, technical, honest about trade-offs, and allergic to marketing fluff.

## The three core traits

### 1. Direct

- No soft openers ("In today's fast-paced world", "Imagine a world where...")
- State the claim or conclusion first, then explain
- Short sentences win. Long sentences only when the idea requires them.
- No unnecessary qualifiers ("I think", "in my opinion", "perhaps")

### 2. Technical (but not exclusionary)

- We are comfortable with technical depth — we are engineers talking to people who build things
- Use concrete examples: specific numbers, real company names (when public), specific tools, actual code
- When simplifying for a non-technical reader, be clear you're simplifying — don't dumb down
- Links to deeper resources for readers who want more

### 3. Honest

- State trade-offs explicitly ("this works well for X, but is wrong for Y")
- Admit what we don't know
- Kill our own bad ideas publicly when the data says so
- "It depends" is a valid answer when it really does depend
- Never exaggerate outcomes or cherry-pick stats

## Banned phrases (immediate rewrite)

**Marketing clichés:**
- "In today's fast-paced world"
- "At the end of the day"
- "Imagine a world where..."
- "Moving forward"
- "Going forward"
- "Leveraging synergies"

**Overused verbs:**
- "leverage" (use "use")
- "unlock" (unlock what, keys?)
- "elevate" (no)
- "transform" (almost always overclaiming)
- "empower" (vague)
- "streamline" (lazy)
- "synergize" (immediate rewrite)

**Overused adjectives:**
- "seamless" (nothing is seamless — tell me what you actually mean)
- "robust" (say what it actually handles)
- "cutting-edge" / "state-of-the-art" / "next-gen"
- "game-changer" / "revolutionary" / "disruptive"
- "innovative" (show don't tell)

**Hollow claims:**
- "Studies show..." without citing the study
- "Customers love..." without a quote
- "X% of companies..." without a source
- "The best..." without criteria

## Preferred patterns

### Instead of "leverage" → "use"
> ❌ We leverage Claude to generate content.
> ✅ We use Claude to generate content.

### Instead of "transform" → describe the specific change
> ❌ This tool transforms your workflow.
> ✅ This tool replaces 4 hours/week of manual tracking with 15 minutes of review.

### Instead of "seamless" → describe how it works
> ❌ Seamless integration with Shopify.
> ✅ One OAuth click connects to Shopify. Orders sync every 30 seconds.

### Instead of "best-in-class" → specific comparison
> ❌ The best-in-class construction CRM.
> ✅ We tested against Procore, Buildertrend, and 3 Indian local tools. Here's the comparison [link].

## Show, don't tell

Our bias is toward:
- **Screenshots** over descriptions of the UI
- **Numbers** over adjectives
- **Customer quotes** over our own claims
- **Code blocks** over prose explanations of code
- **Demo videos** over "how it works" paragraphs
- **Before/after** over "improved"

## Structure patterns

### For blog posts
- **Hook:** a specific observation, number, or contrarian claim
- **Thesis:** one sentence — what the post will prove
- **Body:** 3-5 specific claims, each backed by evidence or example
- **Trade-offs:** honest "when this doesn't apply"
- **CTA:** specific action tied to a product or resource

### For social posts
- **First line:** must earn "show more" / scroll / click
- **Middle:** one idea per paragraph/tweet
- **End:** specific CTA or question

### For product copy
- **Feature headline:** outcome, not feature name
  - ❌ "Advanced Multi-Tenant Architecture"
  - ✅ "Run 50 customer sites with one login"
- **Subhead:** how it works in one sentence
- **Proof point:** number, quote, or visual

## Tone scales by context

| Context | Tone |
|---|---|
| Blog posts | Confident, technical, occasionally opinionated |
| LinkedIn (founder voice) | Story-first, personal, curious, honest about failures |
| X/Twitter | Punchy, sometimes playful, never cringe |
| Customer emails (inbound response) | Warm, helpful, solution-focused, never salesy |
| Outbound cold emails | Specific, brief, curious (asking a question) |
| Support responses | Empathetic, clear, solution-focused |
| Documentation | Precise, example-heavy, assumes minimal context |
| YouTube scripts | Conversational, demo-heavy, specific |

## Emoji usage

- Blog posts: none
- LinkedIn: max 1 per post, usually none
- X: sparingly, only when they add meaning
- Slack/Discord community: normal use, no over-decoration
- Email subject lines: none
- Email bodies: none in cold, rarely in transactional

## Customer-facing specific rules

- Never promise timelines we haven't confirmed
- Never compare to a competitor by name in a negative way without evidence
- Never use "we apologize for the inconvenience" — say what happened, what we're doing, when it'll be fixed
- When things break, be specific about what broke and what's being done — vagueness destroys trust

## Localization notes

We serve global customers. Write in American English as default. When content is specifically for:
- **India:** can use INR pricing, reference local companies/landmarks, but keep English (some audience speaks Hindi but reads English)
- **UK/EU:** prefer spelling if the market is specifically British
- **Other regions:** default to American English unless noted

## Competitive content

When writing about competitors:
- Name them specifically (never "a popular competitor")
- Acknowledge what they do well
- Be specific about our differences, not dismissive
- Back claims with evidence
- Never mock, never punch down, never misrepresent

## Claims and numbers

Any claim with a number needs a source:
- Our own: cite the customer or internal data
- Industry: cite the study/report with a link
- If we don't have a source, delete the claim

## The final check

Before shipping any content, ask:
1. Does the first sentence earn the second?
2. Would I cringe reading this in 6 months?
3. Is there a claim I can't back?
4. Did I use any banned phrase?
5. Did I show specific evidence where I could have?

If any answer is wrong, rewrite.
