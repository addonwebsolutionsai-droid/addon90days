# 100k Users in 12 Months — Strategy & Moat

**Status:** active
**Author:** @cto
**Date:** 2026-05-05

## Context

Founder reviewed competitor https://www.aitmpl.com/ — they advertise 1000+ components (Skills, Agents, Commands, Settings, Hooks, MCPs, Plugins), Featured Integrations, Trending, a Stack Builder, and a Jobs board. SKILON has 130 skills as a single component type. Founder wants us "better than the competitor" and to hit 100k users.

## Why "match every feature" is the wrong fight

Aitmpl.com has BREADTH (7 component types). SKILON has DEPTH (1 type, but Try Live demos, per-skill OG images, Steps/Preview/Overview tabs, 3 install methods, JSON-LD structured data, brand-isolated sister products, India-first content). Two different bets.

Trying to match their breadth requires:
- Building 7 catalog tabs and supporting infrastructure (~2-4 weeks)
- Manually creating ~870 more components (impossible at our quality bar)
- OR letting Skill Smith ship junk to inflate the count (kills trust)

**The cost is wrong, the leverage is wrong, and the win condition is wrong** — even if we matched 1000+ components, we wouldn't hit 100k users from that alone. Counts don't drive users; SEO + virality + retention do.

## Where SKILON wins, ranked by user-acquisition impact

| # | Moat | Why competitor can't easily copy |

## What actually moves 100k users

### Acquisition channels, ranked by realistic 12-month volume

1. **SEO long-tail** — every skill page is a landing page. 130 skills × 3-5 keyword variants = 400-600 keyword positions. With Skill Smith ramping (currently 1/day, ramp to 3/day) we get 500-1000 entries by Day 90. **Estimated: 30-100k organic visitors/month at full ramp.**
2. **SEO category landing pages** — 11 hand-written 500-word pages targeting "claude skills for [category]". Each ranks for medium-tail queries. **Estimated: 5-20k visitors/month per page = 55-220k total at full ramp.**
3. **Social virality from Try Live** — every skill has a OG card + ShareSkill component (X/LinkedIn/WhatsApp/Reddit/copy). Try Live demo screenshot in shared posts converts at high rates. **Estimated: 5-15% of organic visitors share at least once.**
4. **Cross-product pull** — 5 sister product waitlists drive users to /skills. Each waitlist signup = 1 new user. **Estimated: 100-1000 signups per waitlist over 12 months = 500-5000 users baseline.**
5. **Launch-day spikes** — ProductHunt, HN, Reddit, X, LinkedIn (kit already approved). **Estimated: 2-10k users per launch event, 1-3 events.**
6. **Referral loop** — invite-friends already shipped. Per-referral attribution via `?ref=clerkUserId`. **Estimated: 10-30% of users invite ≥1 person who converts.**

### Channels NOT prioritized

- Paid ads (no budget, doesn't fit "free first year" positioning)
- Cold outreach (we already have @outbound-sales for B2B IoT × AI; not for individual users)
- App stores (we're a Claude Code marketplace, not a mobile app)
- Influencer partnerships (until traction signals)

## What we deliberately don't do

- **Don't build a Stack Builder UI** until we have signal that users want it. Static curated bundles first.
- **Don't add a Jobs board.** It's a different product. Distracts from the skills marketplace narrative.
- **Don't degrade Skill Smith quality** to match component count. The quality gate (score ≥ 7) stays.
- **Don't chase competitor features** without measuring whether the gap actually drove their users to us.
