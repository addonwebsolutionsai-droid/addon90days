**Top comment (founder posts immediately):**

Hey HN — founder here.

Context: I run a 10-year-old custom software shop in Ahmedabad, India. Clients across USA, Canada, Ireland, Dubai. Mostly IoT hardware + firmware + enterprise backends. Then AI arrived and I started rebuilding the company as AI-native — this is product #2 of 6 we're shipping in 90 days.

**The problem we're solving:**

Indian SMBs live on WhatsApp. Not email, not Instagram DMs — WhatsApp. A typical kirana (corner grocery), jeweller, or home-delivery food business gets 50–200 WhatsApp messages a day. The owner is manually replying to every single one: price queries, stock checks, order confirmations, payment UPI requests.

This is not a productivity problem. It's an existence problem. These business owners cannot scale because they're personally bottlenecked on their own WhatsApp inbox.

**What we built:**

ChatBase is a WhatsApp AI agent that you connect to your existing WhatsApp Business number (via Meta Cloud API). You feed it a knowledge base — paste your product catalog, price list, FAQs, business hours. It classifies every incoming message into one of 6 intents (price inquiry, order placement, invoice request, payment status, complaint, unknown), then generates a reply in plain conversational Hindi-English from your content using Groq Llama-3.3-70B.

Key design decisions I'll defend:

1. **Complaint intent always escalates (threshold = 1.0).** We explicitly don't try to resolve complaints with AI. The AI sends one empathetic acknowledgement and marks the conversation for human follow-up. Trying to AI-resolve complaints at 70% confidence gets you in trouble.

2. **Unknown intent asks for clarification, does not guess.** Better UX than a hallucinated answer.

3. **Mock mode first.** You can run the full pipeline — create a workspace, add a KB, send mock messages, see AI replies — without a Meta-verified business number. We ran it in mock mode ourselves for two weeks before Meta verification came through.

4. **No vector DB for MVP.** We're doing keyword Jaccard similarity for KB retrieval. Fast enough, works offline, no Qdrant/Pinecone cost during beta. v1.1 will swap to Qdrant.

5. **Groq, not Claude, for this product.** Latency matters on WhatsApp. Groq Llama-3.3-70B is fast and on a generous free tier. Claude is better at nuance but the latency is too variable for a conversational webhook-driven product. We use Claude for everything else.

**Technical stack:**

- Next.js 15 (App Router) + TypeScript strict
- Supabase PostgreSQL with RLS (service-role only on p02_ tables)
- Clerk for auth
- AES-256-GCM encrypted WhatsApp access tokens at rest
- Webhook returns 200 immediately, processes via void async (Vercel Fluid Compute holds the function warm for ~30s post-response)
- 5 tables, 6 seeded intents, all migrations checked in

**What's not in the MVP:**

- Order collection bot (coming in v1.1)
- Appointment booking + Google Calendar OAuth
- Payment reminder sequences
- Bulk customer CSV import
- Editable intents UI (read-only for now)

Free during beta. No card. No waitlist. Create a workspace at the link above and the MockChat widget in Settings lets you demo the full AI pipeline without connecting a real WhatsApp number.

Happy to answer technical questions. We're building in public — the full codebase is on GitHub.
